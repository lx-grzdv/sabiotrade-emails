Hi {{first_name}},

 

Your first SabioTrade assessment has ended.

 

Before you close this email — one thing worth reading.

 

What 'failing' a first assessment actually means:

 

You came to SabioTrade from a trading simulator or demo account. That means you learned to trade in an environment with no real consequences. No daily loss limits. No drawdown rules. No consistency requirements.

 

Then you took an assessment with all of those rules — for the first time.

 

That's not a fair comparison. And it's not a reflection of whether you can trade.

 

What the data shows:

 

The most common reason first assessments end isn't bad strategy. It's one of three things:

 

•    Trading the same position sizes as on a simulator — without accounting for the 5% daily loss limit

•    Holding a losing trade hoping it recovers — instead of cutting it

•    One good day that exceeded 40% of the profit target — which automatically increases the total target

 

All three are adjustments, not talent issues. Traders who come back for a second attempt — knowing what to watch for — pass at a significantly higher rate.

 

What to do differently next time:

 

•    Max position size: 0.5% of account balance per trade

•    Daily loss limit is a hard stop — when you hit 3%, close everything for the day

•    Keep any single day's profit under 40% of your total target

•    Use Sabio Academy before your next attempt — specifically Chapter 4 (Strategy & Risk) and Chapter 5 (Psychology & Discipline)
