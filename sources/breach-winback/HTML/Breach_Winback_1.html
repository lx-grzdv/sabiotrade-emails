What triggered the closure

Either your equity fell 6% below its highest point (Max Trailing Drawdown), or you lost more than 5% in a single day (Daily Loss Limit). Both are common reasons accounts close - not because of bad trading, but because of one moment of overexposure.

What usually causes it:

1. One position that was too large relative to balance.
2. Holding through a news event without a stop-loss.
3. Trying to recover a loss by increasing size after a bad trade.

None of these make you a bad trader. They make you a normal one. The difference between traders who get funded and those who don't is usually one thing: they adjust after a breach.

What funded traders do differently

The traders on our leaderboard who reached funded status share one habit: they capped every trade at 0.5-1% of account balance.

This single rule means 10 losing trades in a row still won't breach the 6% drawdown limit.
