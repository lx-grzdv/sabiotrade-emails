Subject

Your account is up 5% — here's what to do with that

Preheader

Good progress. One thing that keeps it going.

 

Текст письма

Hi {{first_name}},

 

Your {{plan_name}} account is up 5% from your starting balance.

 

That's real progress — and it's worth a moment to think about what's working.

 

What usually happens at this stage:

 

Traders who reach 5% often feel one of two things: confidence that tempts them to increase position sizes, or anxiety that makes them over-manage trades. Both are natural. Both can undo progress faster than any losing streak.

 

What actually keeps the momentum:

 

The same approach that got you to 5%. Same position sizing. Same entry criteria. Same stop-loss discipline. You're halfway to the profit target — the second half is won by not changing what's working.

 

A quick check before your next session:

 

•    Is my position size still capped at 0.5% of account balance?

•    Am I setting a stop-loss before entering every trade?

•    Am I keeping daily profit within the 40% consistency limit ({{daily_consistency_limit}})?

trading_account_daily_loss_over_3pct — дневной убыток -3%

Мгновенно

Subject

Your account is down 3% today — you have 2% left

Preheader

Not a breach. But here's what the next 2 hours look like.

 

Текст письма

Hi {{first_name}},

 

Your account equity is down 3% today.

 

Your daily loss limit is 5% of yesterday's closing balance ({{daily_loss_limit_usd}}). You have 2% — {{daily_remaining_usd}} — remaining before a breach.

 

This is not a breach. But it's the moment where most breaches happen — not from a single bad trade, but from the decision to keep trading after a difficult session.

 

Three options right now:

 

•    Close all positions and stop trading for today. The account is safe. Tomorrow is a clean day.

•    Reduce position size significantly — half your normal size maximum.

•    Take a 15-minute break before touching anything.

 

What the daily loss limit is designed to do:

 

It's a circuit breaker. A 3% down day means something in the market or your execution went wrong today. Trading through that feeling with normal position sizes almost always makes it worse.

 

The traders who pass treat the daily loss limit as a hard stop, not a challenge.

 

You still have tomorrow.

consistency_rule_triggered — profit target вырос

Мгновенно

Subject

Your profit target has increased — here's exactly why

Preheader

One big day moved your target. Here's the math and what to do next.

 

Текст письма

Hi {{first_name}},

 

Your profit target on the {{plan_name}} account has been updated.

 

What happened:

 

You made {{trigger_day_profit_usd}} on {{trigger_date}}. That's a strong day — but it exceeded 40% of your previous profit target, which triggered the Consistency Rule.

 

How the Consistency Rule works:

 

SabioTrade requires that no single trading day accounts for more than 40% of your total profit. When a single day exceeds that threshold, the total profit target is recalculated so that your biggest day represents exactly 40% of the required profit.

 

Your updated numbers:

 

•    Previous target: {{old_target_usd}}

•    Your biggest day: {{trigger_day_profit_usd}}

•    New target: {{new_target_usd}}

•    Already achieved: {{current_profit_usd}}

•    Remaining: {{remaining_profit_usd}}

 

What this means in practice:

 

Your account is still in good standing. The path to passing is the same — consistent daily sessions, controlled position sizing, no single day dominating your P&L.

 

The traders who handle a target recalculation well are usually the ones who pass. Because they understand the rule exists to ensure their funded performance is sustainable — not just lucky.

trading_account_open_loss_tx_7d — убыточная сделка 7+ дней

Мгновенно

Subject

You have a losing trade open for 7 days — here's why that's a problem

Preheader

Holding a loss longer doesn't make it smaller. Here's what the data shows.

 

Текст письма

Hi {{first_name}},

 

You have a trade that's been open and in loss for 7 days on your {{plan_name}} account.

 

Why this matters:

 

Holding a losing trade is one of the most common patterns that leads to assessment breaches. Not because the original trade was wrong — but because of what happens psychologically over time.

 

After 7 days, a losing trade stops being a trading decision and starts being an identity. Closing it feels like admitting a mistake. So it stays open. And it keeps consuming margin, limiting your ability to take other trades, and eating into your drawdown buffer.

 

The math of holding:

 

Your Maximum Trailing Drawdown limit is {{max_drawdown_usd}}. This trade is currently costing you {{open_loss_usd}}.

 

What to consider:

 

•    If your original thesis is still valid — is the stop-loss still in place?

•    If the original stop-loss is gone — the trade is no longer a trade. It's a hope.

•    Closing it now and starting fresh is not a loss. It's capital protection.

 

The assessment doesn't reward patience on losing positions. It rewards discipline.

Мгновенно

Subject

You have a profitable trade open for 7 days — one thing to check

Preheader

Letting profits run is good. But there's a risk specific to prop trading.

 

Текст письма

Hi {{first_name}},

 

You have a trade that's been open and in profit for 7 days on your {{plan_name}} account.

 

Letting profits run is generally correct trading behaviour. But there's one thing specific to prop trading assessments worth checking.

 

The Consistency Rule:

 

If this trade closes in a single day and the profit exceeds 40% of your total profit target, your target will automatically increase.

 

•    Your current profit target: {{profit_target_usd}}

•    40% threshold (max daily profit): {{daily_consistency_limit_usd}}

•    Current unrealized profit on this trade: {{open_profit_usd}}

 

If your unrealized profit is close to or above the threshold, consider:

 

•    Partially closing the position across multiple days

•    Setting a trailing stop to lock in gains while staying within the daily limit

•    Closing a portion today and the rest tomorrow

 

A trade open 7 days also carries overnight risk. Make sure position size is appropriate relative to your current drawdown buffer.
